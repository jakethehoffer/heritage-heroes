# 2026-05-18 hero pitch

## Name
Abraham — the founding patriarch (~1800 BCE). Father of the Jewish people, called by God to leave Ur and start a new covenant.

## One-line hook
The patriarch plays the long game: his Covenant compounds every turn until he's unstoppable.

## Stats (suggested)
- HP: 95 (tested, enduring)
- Attack: 7 (low — peaceful elder, not a brawler)
- Defense: high
- Speed: low

## Special ability
Name: Covenant of Stars
Trigger: Passive — activates automatically at the start of every Abraham turn, no action cost.
Effect: Abraham gains a permanent +2 to his Basic Attack damage each turn (Staff Tap starts at 7 → 9 → 11 → 13 → 15…). Stacks have no cap and persist across the whole match. His active "Special" slot instead holds **Akeidah** (Binding): once per match, sacrifice 15 HP to instantly add +10 permanent stacks to Covenant.
Balance note: Abysmal early-game damage punishes him against burst heroes like Einstein or Moses, who can finish a duel before Covenant matures. Rewards patient defensive play and the Akeidah gamble at low HP for a turnaround. Fills a roster gap — no current hero scales with time.

## Flavor
- Battle quote: "Look now toward heaven, and count the stars — so shall thy strikes be numbered."
- Visual seed: A bearded elder in desert robes leaning on a wooden staff, eyes turned skyward under a deepening night sky. Each turn, another faint star ignites above him until the screen is a constellation.
- Sound seed: A low sustained drone in a minor mode, with a single soft bell-chime layered in each turn — the chimes accumulate into a shimmering chord by late game.

## Why this works
Abraham is the founding patriarch of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam — arguably the single most recognizable name in the entire Hebrew Bible, ahead even of Moses for global recognition. The Covenant mechanic mirrors his defining story beat: God's promise that his descendants would be "as numerous as the stars," a slow-building inheritance that pays off across generations — translated into damage that compounds every turn.
