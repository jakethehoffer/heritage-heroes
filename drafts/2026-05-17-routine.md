# 2026-05-17 hero pitch

## Name
King Solomon — Biblical era (~970–931 BCE), United Kingdom of Israel. Son of David, builder of the First Temple.

## One-line hook
The wisest king in history settles every duel by literally splitting the difference — judgment as a weapon.

## Stats (suggested)
- HP: 90
- Attack: 9 (low — wisdom over force)
- Defense: high
- Speed: medium

## Special ability
Name: Judgment of Solomon
Trigger: Player activates instead of attacking that turn.
Effect: For the next single attack landed by *either* fighter, the damage is split exactly in half (rounded up) and dealt to BOTH fighters. Persists until consumed.
Balance note: Devastating against a charged Einstein E=mc² or Moses' Part the Sea — turns a 40-damage haymaker into 20/20 mutual. But if Solomon throws the next attack himself, he eats half his own Sling-tier output. Skipping his own turn is the cost; reading the opponent is the skill.

## Flavor
- Battle quote: "Cut it in half — and let each take their share."
- Visual seed: A bearded king in a gold-trimmed white robe on a cedar throne, lifting brass scales in one hand and a short sword balanced across his knees. Behind him, the columns of the First Temple rise into Jerusalem dawn light.
- Sound seed: Resonant Levantine harp arpeggio resolving on a struck temple bell; on Judgment, an inhaled silence then a single low gong.

## Why this works
Solomon is the proverbial wise king — son of David (already in the roster, narrative continuity!), builder of the First Temple, hero of the "cut the baby in half" story every grade-schooler learns. The damage-splitting special is a direct mechanical translation of that exact story, and it's the only judgment/share archetype in the lineup — distinct from Esther's reflect, Maimonides' heal, and Golda's double.
