# 2026-05-17 hero pitch

## Name
Bar Kokhba — Roman-era Judea, leader of the 132–136 CE Jewish revolt against Hadrian.

## One-line hook
A doomed messianic warlord whose moves get stronger as the fight gets uglier — glass cannon with a fuse.

## Stats (suggested)
- HP: 90 (mid-range; below Moses/Judah/Golda's 100, above Einstein's 80)
- Attack: 12 (matches David — hard-hitting basic)
- Defense: average
- Speed: high (guerrilla)

## Special ability
Name: Messianic Frenzy
Trigger: Player activates special; lasts 2 turns.
Effect: For the next 2 turns, his Basic Attack strikes **twice** (12 + 12 = 24/turn). During those same 2 turns, all incoming damage against him is increased by 50%.
Balance note: Output ceiling of ~48 damage over 2 turns rivals Einstein's E=mc² (40) but split across turns, so the opponent can defend/counter. The +50% damage taken is the tragic flaw — mistime it against a charged special and he folds. Cannot be reactivated until ability ends.

## Flavor
- Battle quote: "Master of the Universe — neither help us nor hinder us."
- Visual seed: A bearded warrior in torn Roman-issue scale armor, gripping a captured gladius reversed in his hand, eyes lit with feverish certainty. Behind him, the cliffs of Betar in dusk-red light, banners with a star motif snapping in wind.
- Sound seed: Low ram's-horn drone under a quick triplet of struck-iron clangs; on Frenzy activation, a rising vocal "ah" choked off into a war-cry.

## Why this works
Fills the Roman-era gap between Judah Maccabee and Maimonides, and adds the first **double-strike-with-drawback** archetype — distinct from Esther's reflect, Golda's double-next, and Einstein's charge-up. The fuse mechanic makes timing matter: rush Frenzy and you trade big, hold it for a finisher and you risk dying first. Memorable because the special's *cost* is the history lesson — overreach defines Bar Kokhba.
